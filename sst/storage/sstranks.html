A Bit About Ranks
There are several points to consider about ranks, rates, and ratings. They are all important if you wish to get the full enjoyment out of your career. First, ranks deal only with officers, also called commissioned personnel. Rates deal only with enlisted personnel. Ratings also deal with enlisted personnel, but indicate technical specialty, not paygrade. Paygrades are a code indicator of rank and rate. For instance, Admirals are O-8, indicating the eighth tier of the officer category.

Positions have to do somewhat with ratings. It would be rare to find an E-3 with the rating of Electrician's Mate in the Personnel Department, unless he was "wearing more thamn one hat," a phrase commonly used to describe enlisted and junior officers who must operate two or more jobs due to lack of personnel.

Positions CAN BE independent from ranks and rates. A Damage Control team member may not get a promotion for being assigned to the team. Conversely, personnel must attain a certain rank or rate to be eligible for positions. For example, do not request consideration for Platoon Leader if you are not THOROUGLY involved with ship activities, or have a sufficient number of experience points.
